As a beginner, it's important to know how to properly set up your workout routine and make the most out of your gym time. Here are some tips:

  • Start Slow: Don’t rush. Begin with simple exercises, focusing on proper form rather than lifting heavy weights.
  • Warm Up & Stretch: Always start with a warm-up like light cardio and dynamic stretches to prepare your muscles for exercise.
  • Stay Consistent: Aim to exercise 3-4 times per week to start, gradually increasing intensity as you get stronger.
  • Hydrate: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to stay hydrated.

Performing exercises with proper form is crucial to prevent injury and maximize your results. Below are some basic exercises to start with:

Push-Ups

Keep your body straight as you lower yourself down. Push back up to the starting position.

Squats

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your body by bending your knees, keeping your back straight and chest up.

Dumbbell Lateral Raise

Hold the dumbbells at your sides and raise them out to the side until they reach shoulder height.

Alternative Dumbbell Curls

Alternating dumbbell curls, perform one arm at a time. Keep your elbows close to your body and focus on controlled movements.

Barbell Rows

With a barbell, bend over slightly at the waist and pull the barbell towards your lower chest while keeping your back flat.

Tricep Dips

Place your hands on a bench behind you and lower your body by bending your elbows. Push back up to the starting position.

Rest and recovery are just as important as your workouts. Here are some tips:

  • Get Enough Sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night.
  • Active Recovery: Light walking, swimming, or cycling helps maintain blood flow to muscles.
  • Stretch Regularly: Stretching improves flexibility and helps muscle recovery.
